A B S T R A C T   
Identifying modal parameters and updating finite element models (FEMs) of real structures through ambient tests 
is essential in Structural Health Monitoring (SHM). However, efficiently solving the high-dimensional identifi-
cation problem for full-scale structures and rigorously quantifying the uncertainties remain challenging. To 
address these two difficulties, a novel Bayesian framework based on Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is 
developed. The basic idea is to identify the high-dimensional posterior probability density functions (PDFs) in 
both modal analysis and model updating through constructing Markov chains by sampling in the important 
region of the parameter spaces. The sampling is done by Bayes-Mode-ID and the adaptive sequential Monte Carlo 
(ASMC) for modal analysis and model updating, respectively. A new formulation is developed to link the results 
from modal analysis, model updating and model class selection using the Markov chain samples. Thus, the 
propagation of the posterior uncertainties from the identified modal parameters to the updated model param-
eters can be quantified. Furthermore, the original ASMC method, which was developed by the authors, is 
extended for model class selection based on the set of generated Markov chain samples. To demonstrate the 
effectiveness of the proposed framework, multi-setup ambient vibration tests on a busy footbridge were con-
ducted for measuring the accelerations at some pre-selected measurement points. The most probable modal and 
model parameters of the footbridge were identified together with the associated uncertainties by the proposed 
framework.   
1. Introduction 
Structural health monitoring (SHM) is a hot research topic due to the 
demand and development of civil engineering structures. Identifying 
modal parameters and updating finite element models (FEMs) for real 
structures through ambient tests is essential in SHM. Operational modal 
analysis [1] provides modal parameters (e.g., natural frequencies, modal 
damping ratios, and mode shapes) to understand dynamic characteris-
tics of structures so it has been widely used in civil engineering struc-
tures such as towers [2,3], bridges [4–7], and buildings [8–10]. Based on 
the experimentally identified modal parameters, model updating 
[11–16] provides an updated FEM of a structure that better reflects the 
actual structural behaviors. 
Modal analysis and model updating techniques can be categorized 
into deterministic and probabilistic methods. The deterministic 
methods, which mainly focus on finding the optimal values of 
parameters without considering the uncertainties, for modal analysis 
[17–20] and model updating [21–25] have been developed and suc-
cessfully applied in many cases. Even some deterministic methods may 
consider the uncertainties, they are quantified by the concept of 
repeated experiments in the frequentist view. This way of uncertainty 
quantification requires multiple experiments, thus it could be time 
consuming. Moreover, the uncertainties in a frequentist view reflect the 
average of the repeated experiments, which may not be the same as 
the true uncertainties. The probabilistic methods, which mainly focus 
on quantifying the uncertainties, for modal analysis [26–29] and 
model updating [30–35] have been developed and applied in many 
cases. The probabilistic methods are based on Bayesian inference, 
which is a statistical method that provides a probability distribution over 
the parameters of interest. The Bayesian methods can be used to 
quantify the uncertainties in a probabilistic view, which is more 
consistent with the true uncertainties. However, the probabilistic 
methods are computationally expensive and require a large amount 
of data. Therefore, the development of efficient probabilistic 
methods for modal analysis and model updating is highly 
desirable.

MCMC is more flexible in approximating the posterior PDF with 
different degree of complexity in practice. The basic idea is to generate 
samples that constitute Markov chains to explore the parameter space, 
and then the posterior PDF is approximated using the samples [28]. Fang 
et al. [29] used an MCMC-based Bayesian method to update a full-scale 
bridge using field test data. Adeagbo et al. [30] developed a time- 
domain MCMC-based Bayesian model updating method and applied it 
for updating the nonlinear model of a ballast-track system. Ding et al. 
[31] developed a MCMC method for Bayesian updating of a nonlinear 
stochastic model by using the instantaneous characteristics of structural 
dynamic responses. Lam and Hu [32] proposed a parallel MCMC 
method, which was successfully applied in Bayesian model updating of a 
sailboat-shaped building. Among various MCMC methods, the multi- 
level methods with bridge or transition PDFs are worth mentioning. 
For example, Transitional MCMC (TMCMC) [33] and its improvements 
have been successfully developed to approximate complex posterior 
PDFs and the evidence in Bayesian model class selection. TMCMC has 
similar ideas with the ones of the adaptive sequential Monte Carlo 
(ASMC) that is used in the proposed Bayesian framework. These two 
sampling methods differ in how the bridge PDF in each level and the 
proposal PDF for generating candidate samples are constructed. Notable 
TMCMC methods include X-TMCMC [34], the time-domain TMCMC 
[35] BASIS [36] and the parallel TMCMC [37]. 
Despite the progress made in MCMC methods for modal analysis and 
model updating, challenges still remain for full-scale structures. The 
difficulty comes from the large number of measured degrees of freedoms 
(DOFs) required to understand the global structural dynamic charac-
teristics, and the complex FEMs required to well reflect actual structural 
behaviors under operational conditions. One solution to this difficulty is 
model reduction methods that reduce complex FEMs of full-scale 
structures to save computational time. For example, Bayesian sparse 
learning with model reduction by Li et al. [38], model reduction with 
MCMC by Sun and Büyük!oztürk [39] and iterative model reduction 
technique with limited responses by Sengupta and Chakraborty [35]. It 
is possible to integrate model reduction methods into the proposed 
framework to reduce computational time of MCMC where repeated 
evaluation of FEM is required, but this is left for the future work. 
Although using model reduction methods can bring computational ad- 
vantages, it is important to note that the accuracy of the reduced 
models depends on the quality of the original models. Therefore, 
careful model selection and validation are necessary to ensure 
the accuracy of the reduced models. 

The proposed Bayesian framework for modal analysis and model updating 
combines the advantages of both Laplace’s asymptotic approximation and 
MCMC methods. The Laplace’s asymptotic approximation is used to 
approximate the posterior PDF, and the MCMC method is used to generate 
samples that constitute Markov chains to explore the parameter space. 
The proposed framework is computationally efficient and can be 
applied to full-scale structures. The proposed framework can also be 
extended to Bayesian model class selection by using the 
evidence in Bayesian model class selection. The proposed

This paper presents an MCMC-based Bayesian framework for Bayesian modal analysis and model updating of full-scale structures. The proposed framework combines Bayes-Mode-ID and adaptive sequential Monte Carlo (ASMC) to explicitly account for the uncertainty propagation from modal identification to model updating. The framework uses Markov chain samples to build the linkage between the posterior uncertainties of modal identification and model updating. The paper studies the application of the proposed framework on modal analysis of a full-scale footbridge and uncertainty quantification. The results show that the proposed framework can efficiently sample from the complex posterior PDF in a high-dimensional parameter space and explicitly account for the uncertainty propagation from modal identification to model updating.

2.1. The proposed MCMC-based Bayesian framework

inference problem, samples are generated to construct Markov chains to 
explore the parameter space. Once the samples traveling along the 
Markov chains reach the important region of the parameter space, the 
posterior PDF is approximated using the collected samples in that re-
gion. The proposed MCMC-based Bayesian framework uses the Markov 
chain samples to integrate modal analysis, model updating and model 
class selection. Two sampling strategies, Bayes-Mode-ID [40,41] and 
ASMC [44–46], are used to construct the Markov chains for modal 
analysis and model updating, respectively, according to their different 
characteristics. The overall Bayesian framework is developed first (see 
Fig. 1). The sampling strategies are then introduced.

3. Experimental study of a full-scale footbridge 
3.1. Footbridge description 
A footbridge in Hong Kong (see Fig. 4) was used to demonstrate the 
proposed methodology. The purpose is to identify the modal parameters 
under operational conditions to obtain the actual dynamic behaviors of 
this footbridge and update its finite element model for improving the 
modeling of structural characteristics, predicting responses under 
various conditions and structural health monitoring. The details of the 
footbridge are shown in Fig. 5. It is mainly composed of a top truss (see 
Fig. 5 (a)), a bottom truss (see Fig. 5 (b)), two elevation trusses (see 
Fig. 5 (c)), and a concrete slab on the bottom truss. The walking area 
(bottom truss) is approximately 32 m long. It has a varying width with 
the maximum width 6.8 m at the two ends and the minimum width 4.01 
m in the middle. The height between the bottom and top trusses is 3.4 m. 
The thickness of the concrete slab is 0.25 m. The length and width of the 
top truss is about 49.4 m and 7.8 m, respectively. The top and bottom 
trusses consist of multiple types of steel beams, namely, longitudinal, 
lateral and cross beams. The elevation truss consists of steel members 
with circular hollow sections. The six yellow circles represent the loca-
tions that are connected to the piers. The longitudinal beams with 
different colors in the bottom truss are used to distinguish different 
parameterizations in modeling.

updating.

Finally, some discussions about the proposed framework are given. 
The proposed framework is a Bayesian approach to model updating, 
which is a powerful tool for incorporating prior knowledge into 
the model updating process. The proposed framework is also 
flexible and can be applied to different types of models and data. 
The proposed framework is based on the assumption that the 
experimental data is independent and identically distributed (iid) 
with respect to the model parameters. This assumption is 
generally valid for experimental data, but may not be valid 
for numerical data. Therefore, the proposed framework can be 
extended to handle numerical data by using Bayesian inference 
with non-iid data.

4. Concluding remarks 
An MCMC-based Bayesian framework has been developed by 
combining Bayes-Mode-ID and ASMC. The important region of the 
posterior PDF is searched through Markov chains constructed with the 
samples generated by these two sampling methods. A new formulation 
has been developed based on the Markov chain sample to integrate 
modal analysis, model updating and model class selection. Uncertainty 
propagation has been done using this formulation. ASMC has been 
improved with a new formulation that calculates the model class evi-
dence. Multi-setup ambient vibration tests of a full-scale footbridge were 
conducted, and Bayesian modal analysis, model updating and model 
class selection of this footbridge were investigated. The results show that 
the proposed framework is reliable for system identification of full-scale 
structures. 
The posterior uncertainties of the modal parameters and the FEM 
parameters have been studied using the proposed framework. It is found 
that for the footbridge, the posterior uncertainties of the natural fre-
quencies and mode shapes are small compared to those of the damping 
parameters. The posterior uncertainties of the damping parameters are 
significant. The posterior uncertainties of the stiffness parameters are 
significant. The posterior uncertainties of the mass parameters are 
significant. The posterior uncertainties of the length parameters are 
significant. The posterior uncertainties of the section area parameters are 
significant. The posterior uncertainties of the Young’s modulus parameters are 
significant. 
The proposed framework has been applied to a full-scale footbridge 
and a full-scale factory building. The results show that the proposed 
framework is reliable for system identification of full-scale structures.

model reduction techniques can be incorporated into model updating for 
decreasing the order of matrices of FEMs and improving the computa-
tional efficiency of ASMC. The proposed framework does not consider 
the problem of close modes, which may be addressed by using multi- 
mode superposition for the cross-correlation function and conducting 
sampling for the multiple close modes at the same time in the modal- 
component sampling algorithm of Bayes-Mode-ID. Furthermore, the 
proposed framework may be extended to handle non-stochastic sources 
of uncertainty such as environmentally induced changes in dynamic 
behaviors. Modal analysis and model updating can be conducted using 
data on continuous and non-overlapping time windows, and the 
variation of modal and FEM parameters identified on these time win-
dows under different environmental conditions [55–57] is quantified. 
Further research is required along these two directions to enhance the 
applicability of the proposed framework.
